# Creative Principles

## Positioning

The user's direction is atmosphere-first and product-secondary. The goal is to sell a style, taste, emotional state, and visual world. The product does not need to be prominent like a normal product ad.

Do not treat "no obvious product closeup" as a failure by default. It becomes a failure only when there is no other memorable anchor.

## What Must Be Strong Instead

1. Style:
   - The viewer should quickly understand the world: cold, retro, industrial, ritual, street, coastal, luxury, futuristic, or another precise direction.

2. Tribe:
   - Even with multiple people, they should feel connected by the same posture, styling, gaze, pace, and emotional temperature.
   - If the user provides specific models, those people must appear as core characters, not generic extras.

3. Emotion:
   - The film should move through an emotional arc, even if there is no plot.

4. Space:
   - Architecture and location must support the style. A room, street, factory, roof, corridor, or shop must feel chosen, not randomly generated.

5. Symbols:
   - Atmosphere-led work needs repeated visual symbols: color, material, silhouette, object, gesture, light, weather, sound, or camera rhythm.

6. Hiphop gesture:
   - For hiphop-led work, hand gestures and classic body poses can be style anchors.
   - Use head nods, shoulder bounce, hand-to-chest emphasis, open-palm rap gestures, arms crossed, hands in pockets, chain touch, wall lean, low squat, sneaker tap, back-turn reveal, or crew formation.
   - Keep gestures restrained and repeatable; avoid random dancing or accidental gang-sign language.

7. Restraint:
   - High-end AIGC usually improves when motion, effects, and scene variety are controlled.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What exact atmosphere is being sold?
- Who belongs in this world?
- Which provided models must appear in the main frame?
- What repeats enough to become brand memory?
- Which gestures or poses repeat enough to become tribe memory?
- Do scene changes feel like chapters or random jumps?
- Are faces, postures, and styling consistent enough?
- Does each shot deepen the world or merely look nice?
- Does the ending leave a clear visual memory?

## Common Failures

- Random moodboard effect: many attractive shots without one world.
- Character drift: faces, ages, hair, styling, and identity change without intention.
- Missing reference models: supplied models disappear, become background extras, or are replaced by generic AI people.
- Scene drift: unrelated rooms, streets, shops, factories, and rooftops with different visual logic.
- Action fatigue: too much walking, standing, turning, and looking without emotional progress.
- Gesture chaos: too many unrelated hand signs, exaggerated dance moves, or fake rap poses.
- Expression weakness: faces hidden by shadow, sunglasses, side angles, or distance.
- AI plastic feeling: broken hands, unreadable text, fake logos, warped fabric, strange reflections, or unstable architecture.
- Weak ending: the last frame is not the strongest memory.

## Repair Method

1. Choose one atmosphere sentence.
2. Keep only 2-4 location families.
3. Build a character/tribe bible: face type, hair, posture, clothing silhouette, gesture, gaze, and emotional state.
4. Map every provided model to at least one main shot.
5. Choose 3-5 hidden anchors and repeat them.
6. For hiphop films, choose 3-5 gesture anchors and repeat them across shots.
7. Arrange shots into an emotion arc.
8. Cut any shot that does not strengthen the world.
9. End with a clear, iconic image.
