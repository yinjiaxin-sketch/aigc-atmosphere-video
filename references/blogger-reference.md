# Blogger Reference

## Suwen / 稣文

Learn:
- Translate product into brand concept instead of simply showing the item.
- Use space, order, breathing motion, ritual, material texture, and restraint.
- Let architecture and negative space express brand temperament.

Risk:
- Concept can become too abstract.
- Slow rhythm can weaken audience retention.
- Functional product benefits can disappear under atmosphere.

Use for:
- Quiet premium mood, spatial ritual, concept fashion, jewelry, lifestyle objects, and restrained brand worlds.

## Yang Peilin yppl / 杨沛林yppl

Learn:
- Strong Douyin-native hook and fast visual impact.
- Fashion/street/Y2K styling with clear attitude.
- Practical workflow thinking: image references, storyboard, repeatable methods.

Risk:
- Trendiness can overpower brand identity.
- Tutorial value can overpower final film quality.
- Too many visual tricks can compete.

Use for:
- Short-form impact, youth fashion, trend-driven energy, and bold visual hooks.

## Lishen

Learn:
- Cinematic lifestyle and music-video atmosphere.
- Character identity, luxury props, architecture, car, coast, candlelight, costume, and music can build status.
- The product can become desirable through the person and the world around it.

Risk:
- Mood, car, mansion, and props can become more memorable than the brand.
- Luxury symbols can become generic if not tied to the actual positioning.

Use for:
- Identity-based fashion, sunglasses, jewelry, status worldbuilding, and cinematic lifestyle films.

## How To Apply These References

- Use Suwen when the goal is refined spatial atmosphere.
- Use Yang Peilin when the goal is immediate platform impact.
- Use Lishen when the goal is cinematic lifestyle and identity.
- For the user's own work, prioritize atmosphere and style memory over direct product demonstration.
