# Hiphop Visual Authorship

Use this reference when a technically realistic scene still feels generic, empty, commercial, or visually weak.

## First Principle

Realism and beauty are separate requirements.

- Realism asks whether the location, light, material, optics, and movement could physically exist.
- Beauty asks why this exact frame, blocking, spatial rhythm, color hierarchy, and camera attitude are worth remembering.
- Adding smoke, wet floors, cracks, blue-orange lights, grain, or the word `cinematic` does not create authorship.

For this user's hiphop work, stylization should come from composition, performance, edit rhythm, and camera proximity. Do not make the environment look fantastical or glossy to prove that it is stylized.

## Aesthetic Hierarchy

Approve a scene in this order:

1. Silhouette: the frame must still read as a bold graphic arrangement when reduced to black shapes.
2. Spatial motif: select one memorable architecture behavior such as a sweeping ramp, repeated bridge ribs, a narrow daylight slot, a circular pier, or a foreground crash barrier.
3. Character hierarchy: decide who owns foreground, who is revealed, who crosses, and how the crew relationship changes.
4. Light logic: use one dominant environmental source and at most one subordinate practical-light family.
5. Color hierarchy: 70-85% restrained base colors, 10-25% secondary temperature, and at most 5-10% accent color.
6. Surface detail: wear, dust, seams, drainage, and material texture support the frame only after the first five levels work.

If the frame fails at levels 1-3, do not repair it with texture or grading.

## Hiphop Image Grammar

Use a combination of:

- low camera height between 65 and 105 cm;
- wide or normal-wide perspective, usually 24-35 mm equivalent;
- foreground occlusion by a real barrier, pillar edge, fence, doorway, or passing shoulder;
- asymmetrical negative space rather than centered catalog composition;
- one character entering another character's depth plane;
- shoulder-mounted drift, short lateral tracking, low follow, or an intentional locked cypher frame;
- gestures embedded in travel, push-off, crossing, stepping down, crouching, or crew formation;
- hard cuts motivated by body direction, foreground wipes, footfalls, or matching hand positions.

Do not equate hiphop with random hand signs, exaggerated toughness, neon graffiti, luxury cars, constant camera shake, or every character walking toward camera.

## Environment Families

Prefer real, structurally expressive urban spaces:

- a curved concrete vehicle ramp with a narrow daylight opening;
- a flood-control channel beneath a flyover with repeated structural ribs;
- a service yard framed by chain-link, crash barriers, drainage lines, and large piers;
- a basketball-court perimeter or rooftop service deck when it belongs to the same architectural world.

Avoid the generic AI-industrial package:

- endless roll-up shutters;
- decorative random cracks on every surface;
- fully wet mirror floors;
- blue light on one side and orange light on the other without real sources;
- thick smoke that fills empty space;
- many unrelated practical lamps;
- polished concrete, glossy skin, glowing garment prints, or HDR reflections;
- symmetrical fashion-campaign lineup as the default composition.

## Cinematography

Use camera language as behavior, not brand-name decoration:

- 24 fps motion cadence and a 180-degree shutter feel;
- natural motion blur on feet and hands, no fashion slow motion by default;
- restrained handheld inertia, not algorithmic jitter;
- highlight roll-off with protected skin and practical lamps;
- deep shadows with retained concrete and clothing separation;
- matte skin and fabric response, no beauty sheen or wet specular coating;
- no generated film grain. Add stable grain, halation, gate weave, and sharpening decisions in post.

A suitable post target is a restrained 35 mm negative scan character: fine stable grain at low intensity, mild highlight halation only around practical lamps, no crushed blacks, no teal-orange split grade, and no moving grain baked into generated surfaces.

## Scene Test

Before placing a model, verify:

1. The empty frame has one dominant shape and a clear visual direction.
2. At thumbnail size, the frame is recognizable from its architecture, not from effects.
3. The location could be photographed with visible or inferable light sources.
4. The space has three depth planes without filling each plane with clutter.
5. There is an obvious route for the body and camera.
6. Removing all color still leaves a strong composition.

Reject a realistic but forgettable location. Credibility is a gate, not the creative concept.
